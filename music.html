Introduction

My goal by the end of these two months is to learn to solo over jazz tracks with more than just a pentatonic. I chose this goal for the following reasons, in order of importance:

  • It's cool
  • It seems to unlock a lot of other things on guitar. Learning the role of specific chords in progressions, the use of arpeggios, and scales are the fundamental building blocks of most music on guitar. If I have the ability to do this, it should make a lot of other things relatively simple in comparison.
  • I like George Benson

My strategy will be the following:

  • Pick a song
  • Learn the chords
  • Learn the melody
  • Learn the arpeggios
  • Transcribe parts of the solo I like

At roughly an hour per day, I will aim to learn one song every two weeks, increasing to one song per week as the chords and arpeggios become more familiar. I'll post content and videos here to keep myself accountable.

Since we're travelling light, I brought my cavaquinho, and tuned it in the Portuguese style, which is to DGBE - the highest four strings of a guitar in standard tuning. Hopefully this will somewhat translate to guitar once I return :)

Arpeggios

Next, I need to know which arpeggios to play. On cavaquinho this is quite tricky, because you don't actually have a lot of usable notes. Pretty much anything above the 7th fret is tricky and preferably avoidable. This forces you to think in terms of inversions, since you are limited by which root note you can use.

For example, you might want to play c major over the Cmaj7 chord. However, C is at the 10th and 5th frets of the D and G strings respectively. It can be helpful to think modally. For example, we could instead use the natural minor (same notes) from two frets down (8th and 3rd). This contains the same notes (ACEG). Likewise, we can also play E phrygian 8 frets down (from 2nd or 9th), or G mixolydian 5 frets down (5th or open).

So, in order:

  • Em7: E minor melodic (from 2nd fret D string)
  • G9: Ab minor melodic (from 1st fret G string)
  • CMaj7: E phrygian (from 2nd fret D string)
  • F#min7: not sure
  • B7: B mixolydian (from 4th fret G string)
  • F9: F mixolydian (from 3rd fret F string)
  • F#7sus4: no idea
